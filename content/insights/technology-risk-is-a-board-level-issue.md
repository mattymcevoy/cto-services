Technology risk is no longer confined to the IT department.

When systems fail, suppliers miss commitments, data is mishandled, or a cyber incident lands, the impact is commercial and reputational.

## What boards often underestimate

Boards often assume technology risk sits inside operational management.

That is wrong.

Technology risk affects:

- revenue continuity
- customer trust
- regulatory exposure
- investor confidence
- management credibility

## The problem with weak oversight

Where governance is weak, organisations usually show familiar patterns:

- supplier dependence without leverage
- poor visibility of critical risks
- ageing platforms without realistic plans
- no meaningful disaster recovery confidence
- cyber controls that look better on paper than in practice

## What good looks like

Boards do not need technical detail. They need confidence.

That means they should receive:

- a clear view of the top risks
- realistic remediation priorities
- concise reporting on resilience and control
- decision-ready options with trade-offs
- evidence that ownership is real

## Final thought

If the board cannot explain the organisation’s major technology risks in plain language, governance is weaker than people think.
