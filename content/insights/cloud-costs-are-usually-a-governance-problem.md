When organisations complain that cloud is expensive, the diagnosis is often lazy.

Cloud is not usually the root cause.

Poor governance is.

## What drives avoidable spend

Most cost issues come from predictable failures:

- unclear ownership
- weak tagging and reporting
- overprovisioned services
- forgotten resources
- architectural shortcuts
- supplier indifference
- no cost governance rhythm

## Why this matters

Cloud waste is not just a finance issue.

It is a signal that decision-making is weak.

If nobody can answer who owns spend, why it is rising, and what is being done about it, the problem is governance.

## What good looks like

Strong cloud cost control usually includes:

- service and platform ownership
- cost visibility by business or function
- regular review cadence
- architecture standards
- rightsizing discipline
- commercial challenge of suppliers and tooling

## Final thought

Reducing cloud spend is rarely about one-off cuts.

It is about putting operating discipline around technology choices.
