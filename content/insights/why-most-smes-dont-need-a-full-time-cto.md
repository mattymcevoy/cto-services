Most SMEs do not have a technology problem. They have a decision-making problem.

They are often forced into one of two bad choices:

- hire too junior and get activity without leadership
- hire too senior full-time and pay for capacity they do not really need

## The real issue

Technology leadership is not about headcount. It is about clarity.

A business needs someone who can:

- align technology to commercial priorities
- reduce operational and cyber risk
- challenge suppliers and delivery plans
- create a realistic roadmap
- give the board confidence that technology is under control

## Why the full-time model often fails

A permanent CTO makes sense when:

- the company is building technology as a core product
- engineering scale is increasing quickly
- there is ongoing platform or architecture complexity
- technology is central to valuation or investor expectations

But many SMEs are not there yet.

They may need senior technology leadership for:

- one or two days a week
- a transformation programme
- supplier reset and governance
- cloud cost control
- cyber maturity uplift
- pre-investment or post-acquisition stabilisation

## What they actually need

In practice, most organisations need outcomes, not titles.

That usually means:

- sharper decisions
- fewer expensive mistakes
- clearer ownership
- better governance
- lower technology risk

## Final thought

The question is not whether you need a CTO.

The question is whether you need full-time executive overhead, or the right leadership at the right moment.
